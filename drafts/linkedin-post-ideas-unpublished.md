## Post 2: Digital Engineers — the validation gap

90% of engineering teams adopted AI tools. 47% report negative consequences. We're studying what goes wrong.

The productivity numbers don't add up:
→ Simple tasks in the lab: +55% faster
→ Mixed tasks in production: +8% faster
→ Complex real-world engineering: -19% slower

That's a 74-point swing depending on context. The difference isn't the tool — it's whether the engineer can validate what the AI produces.

At HAN I'm investigating this through the Digital Engineers study. What we see so far: 67% of AI suggestions get rejected after review. Validation isn't a side task — it's the main job now. And nobody is systematically studying how engineers actually do it.

We have about 18-24 months before current practices harden into "how it's done." If you're an engineer working with AI, or leading a team that does — I'd like to hear what you're seeing.
