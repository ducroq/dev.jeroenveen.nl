# Where the value is migrating in engineering

*Status: seed note. Captured 2026-05-18 from the practitioner-source collection accumulated through April–May 2026. Different in kind from the other three pieces sketched that day: those (`who-runs-the-drc.md`, `code-native-vs-agent-native.md`, `validation-along-the-stack.md`) are observations about tools. This one is a reading of the practitioner record, anchored in published material by senior engineers at named EU high-tech employers.*

---

## The seed

Several senior practitioners at major Dutch / EU high-tech employers have been publishing about AI in engineering over the past year. None of them are saying the same thing in the same vocabulary. All of them are converging on the same picture once you stack their arguments next to each other.

The picture has three layers:

1. **Upstream.** Problem selection. "Should this thing exist at all?" Restraint, taste, judgment about what is worth building. Lund (Philips) has been writing this layer.
2. **Midstream.** Validation. "Is the AI's output right?" Domain knowledge, critical attitude, the work of checking against an evidence source independent of the model. Rijnsburger (NXP, 2026a) has been writing this layer.
3. **Substrate.** Organisational culture. "Is it safe in this organisation to say the AI is wrong?" Psychological safety as the precondition for the midstream layer to function at all. Rijnsburger (NXP, 2026b) writes this one.

Three layers. Three authors. Independent vocabularies. The same underlying claim: **value is moving away from production and toward the judgment that decides what production is worth, and the judgment that decides whether production was right.**

This is the practitioner-side version of the validation thesis the WHY arc has been articulating. It arrives at the same conclusion from a different starting point: from inside the companies that ship the products, not from inside the development workflows.

## Why this is article-worthy

- The convergence is genuine and not yet named. Each individual practitioner article reads as one person's observation. The three layers stacked together read as an industrial trend.
- It separates the validation thesis from "academic opinion." When senior engineers at NXP and Philips are independently saying the same thing, the writer (Jeroen) is articulating something the field is already approaching, not introducing a frame from outside.
- It belongs on dev.jeroenveen.nl rather than on AE: the AE site is the pattern library, this is a reading of the practitioner record. Different genre. Different surface.
- Per `docs/writing-guide.md` Section 5: the published practitioner articles are public material and are OK to cite. The piece reads the public record. It does not anchor on private conversations or unpublished colleague anecdotes. The line is respected.

## Open questions before drafting

- The collection needs to be substantial enough to be a "convergence" rather than a "list of two." Currently: Rijnsburger 2026a (midstream), Rijnsburger 2026b (substrate), Lund (upstream). That is three articles and two authors. The seed lives in tension: write it now and risk overclaiming; wait for one or two more practitioner sources and risk losing momentum. The right move is probably to write the piece when a fourth source appears and the three-layer frame survives one more datapoint.
- The piece must not anchor on a colleague's lived moment as a load-bearing element (`docs/writing-guide.md` Section 5). Rijnsburger is a personal contact per the Digital Engineer memory; the piece may only cite his published LinkedIn articles, not anything from private conversation. The line is bright. Hold it.
- The three-layer frame is a strong structuring device, but per the writing guide it should not appear as a capitalised label ("the Three-Layer Practitioner Thesis") in the published article. Use the underlying idea without the framework flavour: "they are writing about different parts of the same picture."
- Brand decision is open. The piece reads as dev.jeroenveen.nl writing. If the eventual thought-leadership home is Augmented Engineering or a separate Substack, the same draft probably ports with minor changes. Do not let the brand question delay the writing.

## Reusable phrasings

- *"Three senior engineers, three articles, one picture."*: the structural claim. Strong title candidate.
- *"Value is moving away from production and toward the judgment that decides what production is worth."*: the upstream half of the migration.
- *"And toward the judgment that decides whether production was right."*: the midstream half.
- *"Psychological safety as the precondition for the previous sentence to function at all."*: the substrate. Useful as the third move in a paragraph.
- *"The validation thesis is already in the practitioner record."*: the meta-claim. Useful as either lede or close.
- *"They are writing about different parts of the same picture."*: the non-framework framing of the three layers.
